## What Is Conveyancing?

Conveyancing is the legal process of transferring ownership of real property from one party to another. In Jamaica, this process is governed by the Registration of Titles Act and requires careful attention to documentation, title searches, and stamp duty compliance.

Whether you are purchasing your first home, selling commercial property, or registering land for the first time, understanding each step protects your investment and prevents costly delays.

## The Key Stages

### 1. Agreement for Sale

Once a buyer and seller agree on terms, a formal Agreement for Sale is drafted. This legally binding document sets out the purchase price, deposit amount, completion date, and any conditions of the sale. Both parties must sign and each should have independent legal representation.

### 2. Title Search

Your attorney will conduct a title search at the National Land Agency to confirm:

- The seller is the registered owner
- There are no encumbrances, mortgages, or caveats on the title
- The property boundaries match the registered dimensions

This step is non-negotiable and should never be skipped.

### 3. Stamp Duty and Transfer Tax

The purchaser is responsible for paying Stamp Duty, and the vendor is responsible for Transfer Tax. Your attorney will calculate the exact amounts based on the agreed sale price and current rates set by Tax Administration Jamaica.

### 4. Preparation of the Instrument of Transfer

Once all searches are satisfactory and funds are in order, the Instrument of Transfer is prepared. This formal legal document conveys the title from the vendor to the purchaser and must be executed (signed) before a Justice of the Peace or Notary Public.

### 5. Registration at the National Land Agency

The executed Instrument of Transfer, along with the original Certificate of Title, is lodged at the National Land Agency (NLA) for registration. The NLA processes the transfer and issues a new Certificate of Title in the purchaser's name.

This process typically takes several weeks. Your attorney will follow up and collect the new title on your behalf.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Acting without legal representation** — Attempting to handle a property transfer without an attorney leaves you exposed to fraud, title defects, and procedural errors.
- **Paying deposits without an Agreement for Sale** — Never pay a deposit without a signed legal agreement in place.
- **Ignoring encumbrances** — Mortgages or liens on a property do not automatically disappear at sale. These must be discharged by the vendor before or at completion.
- **Missing deadlines** — The Agreement for Sale sets strict completion dates. Missing these can expose you to penalties or cancellation.
